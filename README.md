# Retail Wine Market Strategy & Consumer Trend Analytics

> Conducted retail market analytics and product strategy modeling to help a local wine retailer adapt to shifting consumer trends, optimize inventory, and improve millennial engagement.

**Author:** Amisha Farhana Shaik  
**Project Type:** Retail Analytics | Market Segmentation | Consumer Strategy | Data-Driven Recommendations  

---

## Business Context

Market Street Wine, an independent wine retailer established in 1979, faced changing consumer behavior due to:

- Rise of natural and organic wines
- Growing millennial purchasing power
- COVID-related supply chain disruptions
- Increased digital influence on buying decisions

The objective was to use data analysis to guide inventory strategy, pricing positioning, and customer engagement efforts.

---

## What I Did

### 1️⃣ Identified Emerging Customer Segments

Analyzed local and global wine trends to segment customers into:

**Experienced Customers**
- Prefer Bordeaux, Burgundy, Champagne
- Open to domestic alternatives due to import constraints

**Millennials**
- Prefer natural, organic wines
- Strong interest in rosé and sparkling
- Price-sensitive (under $20)
- Influenced by social media trends

**Impact:** Enabled dual-segment inventory strategy balancing premium and accessible offerings.

---

### 2️⃣ Evaluated U.S. Wine Region Availability

Analyzed wine-producing regions based on availability of:

- Natural wines
- Organic wines
- Rosé
- Sparkling varieties

Key Findings:
- **California** offers the widest selection (1,750+ options)
- Oregon and New York follow strongly in organic and rosé categories
- Washington and Finger Lakes region show growing potential

**Strategic Insight:** Focus sourcing on high-availability domestic regions to reduce supply risk and increase margin stability.

---

### 3️⃣ Identified High-Quality Wines Under $20

Developed a quality-filtering approach using:

- Expert rating points
- Quality badges (Best Buy, Editor’s Choice, Stellar Selection)
- Vineyard designation indicators

This ensured affordability without sacrificing quality.

**Business Impact:** Strengthened value-based product positioning for price-sensitive customers.

---

### 4️⃣ Analyzed Style & Regional Trends

Focused on rising popularity of Provencal-style rosé (dry, fruity, mineral).

Recommended expanding inventory from:

- Columbia Valley (WA)
- Willamette Valley (OR)
- Finger Lakes (NY)
- Mendocino & Sonoma (CA)
- Monticello AVA (VA)

**Insight:** Lesser-known domestic regions gained traction post-COVID and present strong growth opportunities.

---

### 5️⃣ Developed Growth Strategies

#### Strategy 1: Community Engagement
Revive Friday Night Wine Tastings to:
- Increase in-store traffic
- Build loyalty
- Introduce alternative wines
- Drive experiential retail differentiation

#### Strategy 2: Digital Expansion
Leverage Instagram & influencer-style marketing to:
- Attract millennial customers
- Showcase lifestyle branding
- Increase brand visibility beyond local word-of-mouth

Recommended partnering with wineries that already invest in social media presence.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Retail strategy must adapt to demographic shifts.
- Millennials prioritize affordability, sustainability, and digital influence.
- Domestic sourcing reduces supply volatility.
- Experiential retail + digital marketing creates competitive advantage.
- Data-driven filtering improves product curation and inventory mix.
